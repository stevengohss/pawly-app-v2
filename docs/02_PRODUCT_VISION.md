## Purpose

This document defines why Pawly exists, what differentiates it, and the experience standard that guides product decisions.

## Vision

> To become the place every pet family turns to throughout their journey.

## Core Promise

> Every pet has a story. A different one.

Pawly gives pet parents a private and shareable place to keep moments, care for their pets, reconnect with memories, and find belonging with others who understand.

## What Pawly Is

Pawly is a pet-parent memory and social-life app with practical care tools.

It is not primarily:

- A medical record system.
- A generic checklist app.
- A replacement for veterinary care.
- A generic social feed with pets added to it.

## Core Experience Loop

```text
Something happens with my pet
→ I capture it naturally
→ Pawly makes it worth keeping
→ I decide who sees it
→ It becomes part of my pet’s story
→ Pawly brings it back at the right time
→ I return
```

Pawly also becomes alive when users see, react to, and connect around moments from people and pets they care about.

## Product Pillars

### 1. Moments and Story

Pawly preserves the little and major moments that become a pet’s life story.

### 2. Care With Meaning

Check-ins, reminders, and future care records should feel like support for the pet’s story, not administrative chores.

### 3. Belonging

Friends, community, Lost & Found, events, and future family contributions should connect pet parents through shared care and shared joy.

### 4. Gentle Personalisation

Pawly learns about the pet and user conversationally, then gives meaningful value back.

### 5. Trust and Restraint

Privacy, safety, consent, anti-spam controls, and accurate language matter more than growth hacks.

## Interaction Standard

Pawly should feel like a thoughtful new friend getting to know the pet and the pet parent.

Do not present requests as raw data entry.

Prefer:

- “Who’s this lovely one?”
- “What should we call them?”
- “When do we celebrate Bella?”
- “What would you like Pawly to help you remember?”
- “What happened today that you would like to keep?”

Every question should indicate what it helps Pawly do for the user.

## Emotional Standard

The user should feel:

- “Pawly understands what makes my pet special.”
- “This moment is worth keeping.”
- “This is my pet’s story, not a generic log.”
- “People here understand why this matters.”
- “Pawly noticed something meaningful.”

## Pawly Notices Principle

Pawly may recognise meaningful care, kindness, contribution, or community trust quietly and unexpectedly.

It must not train users to chase points, badges, or repetitive tasks.

## Decision Filter

A proposed feature should satisfy at least one:

- Helps create a moment.
- Helps preserve a moment.
- Helps bring back a moment.
- Helps connect people through a moment.
- Meaningfully improves pet care or safety.

If it only adds data collection, dashboards, or generic utility without a clear user return, it should be reconsidered.

## Long-Term Direction

Later Pawly may expand into:

- Family memory contributors.
- Local pet communities and events.
- Health records and care history.
- Services and partner ecosystem.
- Store and partner rewards.
- Contextual AI guidance.
- Travel and broader pet-life support.

These remain secondary to the core pet-story and belonging experience.
